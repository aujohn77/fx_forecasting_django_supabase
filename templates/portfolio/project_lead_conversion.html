Potential Customers Prediction (MIT IDSS – Capstone Project)

As part of an MIT-IDSS capstone, I built a predictive analytics system to identify leads most likely to convert into paying customers for the fictional EdTech company ExtraaLearn.

I began with data cleaning — removing duplicates, reconciling inconsistencies, validating ranges, and addressing anomalies — followed by univariate and bivariate exploratory analysis.

I engineered features such as engagement scores, interaction ratios, and normalised lead scores to better represent user behaviour, then evaluated multiple models:

  • Decision Trees, Random Forest, Gradient Boosting
  • Logistic Regression, SVM

I applied hyperparameter tuning, threshold optimisation, and precision–recall tradeoff analysis. From the best model, I extracted feature-importance insights and developed lead profiles to support targeted marketing.

Tech Used: Python, Scikit-learn, Pandas, Seaborn
